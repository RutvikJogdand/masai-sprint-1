Crocodiles
Where they are found:
Tropical regions of
  • Asia
  • Africa
  • Americas
  • Australia
4 most dangerous types of Crocodiles:
  • Saltwater Crocodile (Crocodylus porosus)
  • Nile Crocodile (Crocodylus niloticus)
  • Mugger Crocodile (Crocodylus palustris)
  • Black Caiman (Melanosuchus niger)
Cognition:
The crocodile is a pretty smart reptile. Crocodiles possess some advanced cognitive abilities. They can observe and use patterns of prey behaviour, such as when prey come to the river to drink at the same time each day. Crocodiles use twigs as bait for birds looking for nesting material.
Social behaviour and vocalization:
Crocodiles are the most social of reptiles. Even though they do not form social groups, many species congregate in certain sections of rivers, tolerating each other at times of feeding and basking.

Size:
Size greatly varies among species, from the dwarf crocodile to the saltwater crocodile. Species of the dwarf crocodile Osteolaemus grow to an adult size of just 1.5 to 1.9 m (4.9 to 6.2 ft), whereas the saltwater crocodile can grow to sizes over 7 m (23 ft).
